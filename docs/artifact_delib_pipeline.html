Fig. X. ArtifactDelib architecture overview. The framework comprises four tiers. Tier 1 performs multi-expert visual perception: one VisualPerceptionAgent produces a global report, while five domain-specialized experts (Shape, Style, Glyph, MaterialCraft, LocalDetail) each generate focused observations with explicit prompt warnings against anachronistic reasoning (e.g., "a 'Da Ming Xuan De Nian Zhi' mark alone does not certify a Xuande-period authentic piece"). Tier 2 aggregates reports and generates the Top-K (K=3) candidate identifications; a DisagreementAnalyzer then classifies inter-expert分歧 into one of six route hints (SHAPE / STYLE / GLYPH / MATERIAL / LOCAL_DETAIL / MULTI_FACTOR). Tier 3 is the core innovation: a RuleRouter with seven ordered rules maps the disagreement hint to one of seven actions — (①) FAST when confidence ≥ 0.60 and margin ≥ 0.15; (②) a targeted single-expert RECHECK that loops back through re-summarization and re-candidate generation; (③) DELIBERATION when confidence remains low or recheck budget is exhausted. The Controlled Deliberation module pits two HypothesisAgents (each assigned a top candidate) against each other under the supervision of a CriticAgent that judges only whether new discriminative information has surfaced, terminating on MAINTAIN/REVISE/ABSTAIN or after two rounds. Tier 4 is a deferred ArtifactJudge that consumes the original image plus all accumulated evidence (perception, expert reports, recheck history, deliberation transcript) to produce the final natural-language identification. Unlike HistoVerify (Fig. 3), ArtifactDelib requires no parameter training: resource allocation is driven entirely by disagreement semantics rather than learned budget prediction.
